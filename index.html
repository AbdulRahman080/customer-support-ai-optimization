Executive Summary

Customer Support AI Optimization

Dataset  8,469 support tickets Channels  Email · Phone · Chat · Social Approach  Analytics + Gemini API triage prototype

Context

The problem

Manual ticket triage is slow, inconsistent, and agent-intensive. Agents read full descriptions, assign priority, and route cases without decision support — leading to delays, misrouting, and poor satisfaction in high-friction channels.

The approach

Four-stage analysis pipeline: data cleaning and feature engineering, KPI dashboard, friction scoring, and AI triage validation using the Gemini API. Outputs: friction findings, a future-state workflow design, and a channel-specific automation strategy.

Key findings

Highest friction

Refund requests top all friction metrics

Highest delay rate, longest resolution time, and highest customer effort score. Strong candidate for policy-based automation.

Channel gap

Email & social lag behind chat in every metric

Chat delivers the highest satisfaction. Email and social media show the highest friction scores and are the best targets for AI preprocessing at intake.

Priority system flaw

Manual priority labels don't predict resolution speed

High-priority tickets resolved slower than Critical on average. Data-driven escalation prediction outperforms label-based triage.

Recommended next steps

1
Run a 60-day pilot on email triage Deploy AI summarization and routing on the email channel. Measure pre/post handle time, delay rate, and satisfaction to validate the ROI assumptions before broader rollout.
2
Automate refund eligibility checks Map refund policy logic into an eligibility rules engine. Target the ~40% of refund tickets that qualify for straight-through processing — highest friction category, fastest path to agent hour savings.
3
Replace priority labels with a predictive escalation model Train a classifier on resolution delay indicators, ticket type, channel, and customer effort score. Use it to flag at-risk tickets earlier and reduce SLA breach rates.

Estimates are directional. A pilot measurement period is recommended before full deployment.
